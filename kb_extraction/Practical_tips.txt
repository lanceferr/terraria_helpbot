Bosses are aggressive, resilient enemies whose defeat advances the game in some way, such as introducing a new ore for equipment of a higher tier. Each has its own particular way of spawning: some have summoning items that can be used to spawn them manually under certain conditions, while others appear after the player interacts with the environment in a certain way. Events can also have their own event bosses (or mini-bosses) that only spawn during their respective event.

Most bosses and event bosses can pass through blocks of all types, with the exception of King Slime, Queen Slime, and the Flying Dutchman.

All regular bosses besides the Lunatic Cultist have a status message upon spawning, and all regular bosses and invasion event bosses have a status message upon defeat. Each boss has a particular theme that starts playing when they spawn. Event bosses can appear multiple times in a given event and do not have a distinct spawn message. For wave events like the Pumpkin Moon, Frost Moon, and Old One's Army, status messages that appear at the start of each wave can show whether a certain mini-boss will appear.

Bosses appear on the minimap, each with a unique map icon and boss health bar that appear on the screen. The boss health bar only shows the health of the most recently-attacked boss.

There are 33 bosses in total: 8 pre-Hardmode bosses, 10 Hardmode bosses, 14 event bosses, and 1 special world seed boss.

== Pre-Hardmode bosses ==
These are the first bosses the player can encounter. Usually, defeating one of these bosses gives the player items and materials necessary to progress in the game, eventually leading to Hardmode. Although there are 8 pre-Hardmode bosses, only 7 can be spawned in the same world, as the Eater of Worlds is exclusive to worlds containing Corruption, and the Brain of Cthulhu is exclusive to worlds containing Crimson (assuming the player does not place an alternate evil in the same world as the other one, e.g. via seeds from another world or seeds purchased from the Dryad in Hardmode, and does not use a secret seed).

== Hardmode bosses ==
Hardmode bosses are significantly stronger than previous bosses.

== Event bosses ==
Event bosses are classified as mini-bosses, except for the Celestial Pillars. In most situations, event bosses can spawn multiple times during a single event.

== Notes ==
* Boss classification is determined as follows:
** Bosses: boss = true in Terraria's source code;
** Mini-bosses: the other trophy-dropping characters.
* There are several entities that can be considered similar to bosses, but are not:
** The Torch God is an event, but technically classified as a boss in the Bestiary.
** The Dungeon Guardian is almost identical to an enraged Skeletron, yet is certainly not intended to be a boss, with it being far too strong.
** The Dreadnautilus is a special case, as the Re-Logic team had difficulty deciding whether it should be a mini-boss. It was classified as a regular enemy at the end.
* The status messages that appear when the bosses are about to spawn are as follows:
** Eye of Cthulhu: ""
** Eater of Worlds and Brain of Cthulhu: "" after the first and "" after the second Shadow Orb/Crimson Heart is broken. The third only produces a standard summon message.
** The Destroyer: ""
** The Twins: ""
** Skeletron Prime: ""
** Mechdusa: ""
** Moon Lord: ""
* As of 1.2.3, worlds generated in earlier versions, in which a player defeated all mechanical bosses before upgrading, will not have those bosses register as defeated; for instance, Plantera's Bulbs will not spawn, and the Steampunker will not arrive.
